Notes from a Walk-Through

  • EC2: 24 instances; 8 × m5.2xlarge at 9% avg CPU. No Savings Plan.
  • RDS: 4 Aurora clusters; 2 dev clusters running 24×7
  • S3: 14 TB of legacy logs in Standard, never accessed
  • EBS: 38 unattached gp2 volumes, 4.2 TB
  • NAT Gateway: Lambda → S3 traffic via NAT (no VPC endpoint)
  • Logs: No retention set, infinite by default
  • $1,800 untagged — nobody can attribute

Tasks

  1. Identify the top 5 sources of waste — one sentence each
  2. Pick the 3 fixes you'd do first — justify by impact & effort
  3. Estimate annual savings for your top three
  4. Propose one rule that prevents the $1,800 untagged spend going forward

One Possible Set of Findings

  • Right-size or consolidate the 8 idle m5.2xlarge → 50–70% on those nodes
  • 1-yr Compute Savings Plan on baseline → ~25–30% on covered usage
  • Schedule dev/staging RDS off nights/weekends → ~60% on those clusters
  • Tier 14 TB cold logs to Glacier → ~80–95% on that storage
  • Delete unattached EBS & old snapshots
  • S3 Gateway VPC endpoint to bypass NAT charges
  • CloudWatch Logs retention policy
  • Required tags via SCP / Config rule

Discussion

  • Which fixes are one-time vs. ongoing?
  • Engineering work vs. finance/procurement decision?
  • How do you avoid backsliding three months from now?
