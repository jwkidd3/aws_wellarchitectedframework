Pillar 6

Sustainability

Minimize the environmental impact of your cloud workloads.

Shared Responsibility

  • AWS is responsible for sustainability of the cloud — power sources, hardware efficiency, data center cooling
  • You are responsible for sustainability in the cloud — what you provision and how efficiently it runs
  • Goal: more useful work per unit of energy

Region Selection

  • Some AWS Regions run on a higher mix of renewable energy than others
  • For work that isn't latency-sensitive (nightly batch jobs, analytics), pick a greener region
  • Balance against latency, data residency laws, and cost

Why the Cloud Can Be Greener

  • AWS data centers run at much higher utilization than most on-prem ones — less idle hardware per unit of work
  • Hyperscale cooling and power delivery is dramatically more efficient than small server rooms
  • AWS is investing in renewable power for its regions; some regions are well ahead of others
  • None of that matters if your workload runs 100 idle EC2 instances 24×7 — the customer side of the shared responsibility is where most of the leverage lives

Efficient Resource Use

  • Right-sizing is both a cost and sustainability practice
  • High utilization beats idle capacity
  • Auto Scaling and serverless raise utilization by default
  • Graviton — AWS's own ARM-based CPUs that deliver more work per watt than equivalent Intel/AMD instances

Operational vs. Embodied Carbon

  • Operational carbon — energy used while running the workload (electricity, cooling)
  • Embodied carbon — emissions from manufacturing the hardware itself
  • You reduce operational carbon by running less, smarter, and on cleaner power
  • You reduce embodied carbon by extracting more work from each physical server — i.e., raising utilization
  • High utilization helps both — which is why auto-scaling and serverless are sustainability wins

Measuring Sustainability

  • Customer Carbon Footprint Tool — AWS's built-in report of your estimated emissions from AWS usage
  • Available in the Billing console; updated monthly
  • Doesn't cover every workload detail, but gives a directional trend
  • Track it the way you'd track any other SLO — set a target, review quarterly

Data Management

  • Storage tiering — move rarely-accessed data to cheaper, lower-energy storage classes
  • S3 Intelligent-Tiering — moves objects between classes automatically
  • S3 Glacier — very cheap archival storage (retrieval takes minutes to hours)
  • Lifecycle policies — auto-expire or auto-archive objects after N days
  • Don't store what you'll never read back

Architecture Patterns

  • Asynchronous & queue-based — smooths bursty load, raises utilization
  • Edge caching via CloudFront — fewer origin requests, less long-haul traffic
  • Efficient code is efficient energy: a 2× speedup is a 2× efficiency gain
